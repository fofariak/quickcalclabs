What are Calories and TDEE?

A calorie is a unit of energy. In nutrition, calories refer to the energy people get from the food and drink they consume, as well as the energy they use in physical activity. Calories are essential for human health; the key is consuming the right amount.

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour period. It's composed of three main components:

  • Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): The energy your body uses at rest for basic functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. This is the largest component of your TDEE.
  • Thermic Effect of Food (TEF): The calories burned during the digestion and processing of food.
  • Physical Activity: The energy used during exercise and daily movements (from walking to intense workouts).

Understanding Your Calorie Goals

Our calculator provides three main targets based on your TDEE:

  • üî• Cutting (Weight Loss): To lose weight, you need to be in a calorie deficit, meaning you consume fewer calories than your TDEE. Our calculator suggests a moderate deficit (typically 500 calories less than maintenance) for sustainable fat loss of about 1 lb per week.
  • üí™ Bulking (Weight Gain): To gain weight (ideally muscle), you need to be in a calorie surplus. Our calculator suggests a modest surplus (typically 500 calories more than maintenance) to promote lean muscle gain while minimizing fat gain.

The Science Behind the Calculation

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). Published in 1990, it is considered one of the most accurate BMR formulas. It is more reliable than the older Harris-Benedict equation. The formula is:

  • For Men: BMR = 10 * weight (kg) + 6.25 * height (cm) - 5 * age (y) + 5
  • For Women: BMR = 10 * weight (kg) + 6.25 * height (cm) - 5 * age (y) - 161

Your BMR is then multiplied by an activity factor to determine your TDEE (maintenance calories).

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this calorie calculator?

Our calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is widely considered by experts to be one of the most accurate methods for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). However, it's still an estimate. Individual metabolism can vary, so it's best to use this as a starting point and adjust based on your results.

Why do I need to enter my activity level?

Your activity level is a crucial factor in determining your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). Your BMR is the calories you burn at rest, but any movement‚Äîfrom walking to intense exercise‚Äîburns additional calories. The activity multiplier helps to account for this, providing a more realistic daily calorie target.

How fast can I expect to lose weight on a cutting diet?

A safe and sustainable rate of weight loss is generally considered to be 1-2 pounds (about 0.5-1 kg) per week. This typically requires a daily calorie deficit of 500-1000 calories. Losing weight faster than this often leads to muscle loss and is harder to maintain long-term.

Should I eat the same number of calories on rest days?

For simplicity and consistency, most people eat the same number of calories each day. However, some advanced individuals practice "calorie cycling," where they eat more calories on training days and fewer on rest days. For most people, maintaining a consistent daily average is effective and easier to manage.
