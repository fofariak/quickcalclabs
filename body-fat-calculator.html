‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the US Navy body fat method?

The US Navy method is quite accurate with an error rate of ¬±3-4%. While not as precise as DEXA scans, it's one of the most reliable measurement-based methods available and correlates well with more expensive testing methods.

Why do women need hip measurements but men don't?

Women typically store more fat in their hips and thighs due to hormonal differences. The hip measurement helps account for this gender-specific fat distribution pattern, making the calculation more accurate for females.

What's a healthy body fat percentage?

Healthy ranges vary by gender and fitness level. For men, 10-18% is generally considered healthy, while for women, 16-25% is typical. Athletes often have lower percentages, while some body fat is essential for hormone production and organ protection.

Can I use this calculator for weight loss tracking?

Yes! üí™ Body fat percentage is excellent for tracking fitness progress because it shows changes in body composition, not just weight. You might maintain the same weight while losing fat and gaining muscle.

How often should I measure my body fat?

For tracking progress, measure monthly or bi-weekly. Daily fluctuations are normal due to hydration, food intake, and other factors. Consistent measurement conditions (same time of day, similar hydration) improve accuracy.
