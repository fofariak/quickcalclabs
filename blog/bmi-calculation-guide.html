How to Calculate BMI at Home (With Examples & Free Online Calculator)

Published: August 26, 2025 • QuickCalcLabs

BMI is an easy, widely used measure to assess weight status relative to height. Use our free BMI Calculator for instant results and a smart interpretation of your number.

Formulas (Metric & Imperial)

  • Metric: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height(m)²
  • Imperial: BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height(in)²) × 703

Interpreting BMI

Under 18.5 = underweight; 18.5–24.9 = normal; 25–29.9 = overweight; 30+ = obesity. Remember: BMI is a screening tool — muscle mass, bone density, and body composition matter.

Example

150 lbs, 5'6" → height = 66 in → BMI = (150 ÷ 66²) × 703 = 24.2 (Normal).
