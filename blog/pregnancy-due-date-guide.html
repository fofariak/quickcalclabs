How to Calculate Your Due Date & Track Pregnancy Weeks

Published: August 26, 2025 • QuickCalcLabs

Estimating your due date gives structure to prenatal care and planning. Our Pregnancy Calculator uses standard rules to estimate due date and week-by-week milestones.

Naegel’s Rule — quick method

Naegel’s Rule: add 1 year, subtract 3 months, then add 7 days to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). This gives an estimated due date (EDD).

What to expect by trimester

  • First trimester (0–13 weeks): early development, common nausea; initial screening tests.
  • Second trimester (14–26 weeks): quickening (baby movement), anatomy scan.
  • Third trimester (27–40 weeks): growth monitoring and birth planning.

Why calculators matter

Online tools standardize the math and help you visualize week-by-week progress. Our Pregnancy Calculator displays milestones and an EDD, and it’s mobile friendly.

Note: Only ~5% of births occur on the exact EDD. Use the estimate for planning; follow clinical guidance for personalized care.
