1) Why Weight Matters for Pets

Weight affects mobility, heart health, and lifespan. Maintaining a healthy range reduces the risk of arthritis, diabetes, and reduced activity.

Even a 10‚Äì15% increase above ideal weight can raise health risks. Excess weight increases joint strain and can worsen breathing issues, while low weight can signal disease or inadequate nutrition.

BCS is a simple 1‚Äì9 scale. Ideal pets have visible waists, ribs that are easily felt (not seen), and a defined abdominal tuck.

When you gently run your hands along your pet‚Äôs ribs, you should feel them easily with a light fat covering. If you must press firmly, weight is likely high. If ribs are very visible, weight may be too low.

3) Healthy Weight Ranges

Breed, age, and frame size matter. Use weight charts as guidance, then adjust with your veterinarian to match your pet‚Äôs unique build.

Breed charts are guidelines, not rules. Two dogs of the same breed can have different ideal weights depending on frame size and muscle mass.

4) Red Flags to Watch

  • Rapid weight gain or loss
  • Loss of muscle tone
  • Reduced energy or play interest

Adjust portions gradually, increase low‚Äëimpact activity, and measure progress weekly. Avoid dramatic restriction, especially for cats.

Safe weight change is gradual. For most pets, aim for 1‚Äì2% of body weight per week. Combine portion adjustment with light exercise rather than aggressive restriction.
