How Many Calories Do You Need Per Day? TDEE & Calorie Calculator Guide

Published: August 26, 2025 • QuickCalcLabs

Calories fuel everything you do. To plan weight changes or maintain health, start with your BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) and multiply by activity to get TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure). Use our Calorie Calculator for a precise result.

BMR & TDEE explained

BMR is the calories your body needs at rest. Common formulas (Mifflin–St Jeor) use weight, height and age. TDEE = BMR × activity factor (sedentary → very active).

Example

Male, 30y, 77 kg, 175 cm, moderately active (1.55): Mifflin–St Jeor → BMR ≈ 1,700 kcal → TDEE ≈ 2,635 kcal/day.

Use it to plan goals

For weight loss, create a sustainable deficit (e.g., 10–20%). For muscle gain, use a modest surplus. Combine calorie targets with our Macro Calculator for daily grams.
