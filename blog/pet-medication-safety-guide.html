Dogs and cats have different metabolisms, sizes, and sensitivities. A dose that is safe for one species or weight class can be dangerous for another.

Some OTC medicines are toxic to pets. Even small amounts of ibuprofen, acetaminophen, or cold medicines can cause severe harm.

Red Flag: Never give ibuprofen, acetaminophen, or human cold meds to pets unless a veterinarian explicitly instructs you to.

Another common error is stopping antibiotics early when symptoms improve. This can lead to relapse and resistance. Finish the course unless a vet advises otherwise.

5) Side Effects and Red Flags

Watch for vomiting, lethargy, loss of appetite, or unusual behavior. If symptoms are severe or sudden, seek veterinary care immediately.

Subtle signs like reduced appetite, hiding, or changes in gait can indicate a medication issue. Keep a simple log of dosage time and symptoms to share with your vet.

6) Safe Storage & Scheduling

Pro Tip: Use a consistent schedule and a visible tracking system to reduce missed or double doses.

Store medications out of reach and away from heat or humidity. Clear labeling prevents confusion between multiple pets or prescriptions.
