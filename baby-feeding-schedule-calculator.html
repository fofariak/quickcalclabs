How to Use the Baby Feeding Schedule Calculator

Understanding your baby's nutritional needs is a key part of parenting. This calculator provides a sample feeding schedule based on general guidelines for a baby's age and weight. Simply enter your baby's details to see an estimate of their daily milk intake, how often they might feed, and how much they might drink at each feeding.

Important considerations:

  • This is an estimate. Every baby is different. Always follow your baby's hunger cues (like rooting, sucking on fists, and crying) rather than sticking rigidly to a clock.
  • Breastfed babies may eat more frequently and in varying amounts compared to formula-fed babies. "On-demand" feeding is the standard for breastfeeding.
  • Growth spurts will temporarily increase your baby's appetite. Be prepared for more frequent or larger feedings during these periods.

How much milk does my baby need?

A general guideline is that babies need about 2.5 ounces of formula or breastmilk per pound (or 150-200 mL per kg) of body weight per day. This calculator uses this principle to create its estimate.

How do I know if my baby is getting enough milk?

Look for reliable signs: consistent weight gain along their growth curve, 6-8 wet diapers per day, and seeming content and satisfied after feedings. If you have any concerns, always consult your pediatrician.

Can I overfeed my baby?

It is difficult to overfeed a breastfed baby, as they are very good at self-regulating. It is possible to overfeed a bottle-fed baby. To avoid this, practice "paced bottle feeding," where you hold the bottle horizontally and allow the baby to take breaks, mimicking the flow of breastfeeding.
